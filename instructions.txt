Miner overhaul:

Let’s create a separate plugin for the miner overhaul.  Its classes will be imported into specialization and vice versa.

The goal:

Miners are able to choose a single “sub class” that allows prospecting for a given ore. Once the player picks a sub class, he cannot change, even if he dies/loses levels and later regains a miner level. The sub class selection will be available once player levels up to the appropriate miner level. It should be displayed as text in chat.

Upon leveling miner (check specialization plugin’s cheer message and hook into there): “You may sub class into [Ore 1] Prospecting, [Ore 2] Prospecting, … , OR [Ore X] Prospecting. Note: You cannot change your subclass once you have selected it.”

Hovering over the “[Ore] Prospecting” will show a description of the sub class, followed by “click to select”. 
After clicking, another text in the chat is shown “Are you sure you want to choose [Ore] Prospecting? Subclass cannot be changed once selected. <Yes> <No>”

If player clicks yes:
“You have selected [Ore] Prospecting”.
If clicks no, then “No subclass selected. If you change your mind, you may use /subclass miner [ore] to select a subclass”. 

Level 1 miner can choose Coal Prospecting. 
Level 2 miner can choose Copper Prospecting or previous levels’ prospecting.
Level 3 miner can choose Gold prospecting OR Gem Prospecting, or previous levels’ prospecting. Gem prospecting includes redstone, emerald, and lapis.
Level 4 miner can choose Iron prospecting or previous levels’ prospecting..
Level 5 miner can choose Diamond prospecting or previous levels’ prospecting..

Prospecting: right click with a pickaxe to “scan” for nearby ores. Prospecting should be displayed as text in the chat. Will scan within 100 blocks in the x z plane each direction of the player, and detect if there are any ore veins in the area. 

“Found nothing of interest” is reported when no ore veins found in the scan. 
Proximity is reported on prospecting: 
“Found traces of [ore]” is given when the “straight line” distance (in x y z coordinates) between player and ore vein is over 75 blocks.
“Found small sample of [ore]” is given when distance is less than 75 but greater than 40.
“Found medium sample of [ore]” is given when distance is less than 40 but greater than 15.
“[size] [ore] vein is detected close by” is given when distance is less than 15.

For size of vein, we will use “small”, “average”, and “large”. For the actual size (block count), we will refer to the datapack. 
Note: these distances should be configurable in the plugin’s config.yml

Example:
<Coal Prospecting> Found nothing of interest.
<Gem Prospecting> Found traces of lapis lazuli. 

Additional goal:

Let’s configure the BDR datapack to add configured features for ore veins. There will be 3 sizes:
Tiny = ~200 ore blocks
Small = ~400 ore blocks
Average = ~600 ore blocks
Large = ~1000 ore blocks
Coal and copper ore will have either average or large sizes.
Gold, lapis, redstone, and emeralds will have tiny, small, and average sizes.
Iron can have any size.
Diamond will only have tiny size.

Ore veins are blob shaped. Each ore vein will have 5-8 block long “streaks” of smaller veins coming from the central blob in all directions (like rays from the sun).
